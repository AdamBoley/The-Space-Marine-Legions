The Traitor Legions

Though they may be foul traitors to the Imperium of Mankind, The Nine Who Turned are still military forces with rich histories, interesting combat doctrines and unique combat formations. These combined to make a fractious coalition that came within spitting distance of overthrowing the Imperium. See below for detailed breakdowns of these infamous formations.

3rd Legion Emperor's Children

Quick Facts

  • Name: The Emperor's Children
  • Primarch: Fulgrim, The Phoenician
  • Bodyguard: The Phoenix Guard
  • Homeworld: Chemos
  • Flagship: Pride of the Emperor
  • Organisation: Millenials > Cohorts > Companies
  • Strength: 110,000

History

The Emperor's Children were initially employed as equerries and bodyguards to officials. They also led Imperial armies into battle

The men of the III Legion were used as equerries and diplomatic bodyguards. They also bore Imperial standards into battle when leading the armies of newly conquered worlds into battle. This kept said armies in line. This mission as honour guards had them adopt the Legion's later customary Imperial Purple colour scheme. After the Proximan Betrayal, in which the XVI Cohort were slain to a man defending the Emperor and his Standard, the Legion was granted the honour of bearing the Palatine Aquila on their chest-plates, an honour not granted to any other Legion.

Shortly after the start of the Great Crusade, and after the Battle of Proxima, the III Legion's gene-seed reserve was lost in an incident on Luna. An unknown virus infected the gene-seed reserves on Terra shortly after, destroying the entire gene-seed reserve, almost eliminating the ability of the Legion to replenish its numbers. By the time Chemos was found and Fulgrim reunited with his Legion, they numbered only 200 strong. At this time, Fulgrim gave the Legion its current cognomen. To rebuild, the Legion recruited from the nobility of newly compliant worlds. To aid their rebuilding, the Legion served alongside the XVI Legion, with Horus mentoring Fulgrim until his Legion was strong enough to operate independently. The Legion set out to learn and master all aspects of warfare, once demonstrating Guilliman's counter-attack doctrines better than the Ultramarines had.

Observations

The Emperor's Children favour speed and mobility in battle, and are excellent at combined-arms warfare

The Legion's mentality of perfection causes them to be wounded when another Legion is honoured. They are jealous of their betters and contemptuous of their lessers. Certain martial virtues are thought of as superior, and Fulgrim's opinions and inclinations filter down through the ranks to become ironclad doctrine. This indicates a certain rigidity of thought.

One such doctrine is a focus on speed and manoeuvre warfare, and the Legion makes extensive use of jump-packs, Land Speeders and jetbikes. Speed is favoured over firepower, endurance and strength, with the Legion believing, perhaps not erroneously, that the victorious warrior is he who strikes first. This is driven in part by the lack of numbers and a disinclination to engage in attritional warfare. The Legion is famed for its planning and analysis of every aspect of battle. Though a very useful mindset, they can be wrong-footed when the fog of war disrupts battle plans. The Legion also maintains an unusually well staffed and well equipped Apothecarion.

The Legion is organised rigidly, with clear but strict lines of authority and hierarchy. Changes are not made on a spur-of-the-moment basis, but only after careful deliberation. Their culture emphasises that every warrior knows his place. Marks of honour and renown are gifted to warriors by superiors, rather than being assumed by those warriors.

After Isstvan V, Horus let slip the Legion to wreak havoc on the Imperium. The Legion leads the charge in hunting down elements of the Shattered Legions.
